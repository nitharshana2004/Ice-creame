Stick ice-Creame

An ice cream bar is a frozen dessert on a stick. It features ice cream, distinguishing it from an ice pop, which does not contain any ice cream. Ice cream bars are often dipped and covered in chocolate. The ice sticks to the skin due to the formation of strong hydrogen bonds between water molecules in the ice and those on the skin. If ice sticks to your fingers, it will usually fall off quickly, due to your body's warmth.
